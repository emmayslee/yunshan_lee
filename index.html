About Me

Research-driven.
Product-minded.

I'm a graduate student at Boston University's Questrom School of Business, completing an M.S. in Management (STEM track).

I've led end-to-end research across AI products, B2B enterprise tools, and consumer apps — conducting stakeholder interviews, usability studies, and journey mapping, then translating findings into clear product recommendations. My work sits at the intersection of user research and product strategy — I help teams understand not just what users do, but why they do it, and what to build next.

Before UX research, I spent two years as an English tutor — where I first learned to read people: observing behavioral patterns, adapting approaches, and iterating on what actually worked. That instinct carried into how I work today — I don't just surface insights. I build what comes next.
